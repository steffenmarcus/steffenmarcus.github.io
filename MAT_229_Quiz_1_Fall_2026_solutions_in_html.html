1)a) Consider the points \(P(1, 3, 3), Q(2, 5, 5),\) and \(R(6, 7, 12)\) in \(\mathbb {R}^3\). Compute the vectors \({\bf a} = \overrightarrow {PQ}\) and \({\bf b} = \overrightarrow {PR}\).

To find the vector from point \(P\) to point \(Q\), we compute: \begin {align*} {\bf a} = \overrightarrow {PQ} &= \overrightarrow {OQ} - \overrightarrow {OP} \\ &= \langle 2, 5, 5 \rangle - \langle 1, 3, 3 \rangle \\ &= \langle 2-1, 5-3, 5-3 \rangle \\ &= \langle 1, 2, 2 \rangle \end {align*}

Similarly, to find the vector from point \(P\) to point \(R\): \begin {align*} {\bf b} = \overrightarrow {PR} &= \overrightarrow {OR} - \overrightarrow {OP} \\ &= \langle 6, 7, 12 \rangle - \langle 1, 3, 3 \rangle \\ &= \langle 6-1, 7-3, 12-3 \rangle \\ &= \langle 5, 4, 9 \rangle \end {align*}

Therefore, \({\bf a} = \langle 1, 2, 2 \rangle \) and \({\bf b} = \langle 5, 4, 9 \rangle \).

1)b))Consider the vectors \({\bf a}=\langle 9, 8, 7 \rangle \) and \({\bf b}=\langle 7, 0, -9 \rangle \). Perform the following computations:

(i) \({\bf a} + {\bf b}\)

We add the vectors component-wise: \begin {align*} {\bf a} + {\bf b} &= \langle 9, 8, 7 \rangle + \langle 7, 0, -9 \rangle \\ &= \langle 9+7, 8+0, 7+(-9) \rangle \\ &= \langle 16, 8, -2 \rangle \end {align*}

(ii) \({\bf 2a} - {\bf 3b}\)

We first scalar multiply each vector, then subtract: \begin {align*} {\bf 2a} - {\bf 3b} &= 2\langle 9, 8, 7 \rangle - 3\langle 7, 0, -9 \rangle \\ &= \langle 18, 16, 14 \rangle - \langle 21, 0, -27 \rangle \\ &= \langle 18-21, 16-0, 14-(-27) \rangle \\ &= \langle -3, 16, 41 \rangle \end {align*}

(iii) \(| {\bf a} |\)

The magnitude of \(\bf a\) is computed using the distance formula: \begin {align*} | {\bf a} | &= \sqrt {9^2 + 8^2 + 7^2} \\ &= \sqrt {81 + 64 + 49} \\ &= \sqrt {194} \end {align*}

(iiii) The unit vector \(\bf u\) in the direction of \(\bf a\).

A unit vector in the direction of \(\bf a\) is found by dividing \(\bf a\) by its magnitude: \begin {align*} {\bf u} &= \frac {{\bf a}}{|{\bf a}|} \\ &= \frac {\langle 9, 8, 7 \rangle }{\sqrt {194}} \\ &= \left \langle \frac {9}{\sqrt {194}}, \frac {8}{\sqrt {194}}, \frac {7}{\sqrt {194}} \right \rangle \end {align*}

Alternatively, we can rationalize the denominator: \begin {align*} {\bf u} &= \left \langle \frac {9\sqrt {194}}{194}, \frac {8\sqrt {194}}{194}, \frac {7\sqrt {194}}{194} \right \rangle \end {align*}

Either form is acceptable.
